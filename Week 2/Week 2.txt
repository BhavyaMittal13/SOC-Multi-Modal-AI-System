📝 Summary of Learning
This week felt like the real beginning of ML. We finally wrote code that trains a model! Key things that clicked:
* Pandas is like a spreadsheet inside Python. head(), describe(), isnull() — these three alone are incredibly useful.
* Train-test split is one of those things that seems obvious once you understand it but is crucial to get right. You can never evaluate a model on data it was trained on.
* Linear Regression is surprisingly interpretable. The coefficients directly tell you "if X increases by 1, Y increases by coef".
* R² Score confused me at first. An R² of 0.85 means the model explains 85% of the variance in the data. Negative R² means the model is worse than just predicting the mean every time.
One thing I struggled with: understanding what "normalization" actually does mathematically. I get that it scales features to similar ranges, but I'm not fully sure why it always helps. Will dig deeper.

💡 Key Takeaways
1. Data cleaning takes more time than modeling — real datasets are messy.
2. Always check df.info() and df.describe() before doing anything with data.
3. Never evaluate a model on training data.
4. Linear Regression works well when the relationship between variables is actually linear.
5. R² > 0.8 is generally considered a good fit for simple regression problems.
6. Overfitting = great training score, poor test score. Always compare both.
